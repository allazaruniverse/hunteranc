The Recon(CHA/STR)

"Silent Eyes, Decisive Strikes."

"From the shadows, I see everything—guiding every move, marking every threat, and ensuring victory before the first shot is fired."

Overview

The Intimidator is the muscle & the enforcer using their strength, and and charisma to dominate others and control the battlefield. They aren’t just about brute force—they know how to use their presence and fearsome reputation to manipulate situations, coerce others into submission, and get the job done. Whether it’s making an enemy cower in fear or persuading someone to give up vital information, the Intimidator gets results through a blend of charisma and raw power.

Think of The Intimidator as a character who can crush skulls with their fists but also intimidate a room full of enemies into submission without breaking a sweat. They are the heavy hitter both physically and socially.

The Intimidator excels at controlling situations through fear, force, and sheer presence. Whether it’s breaking bonesor breaking spirits, they ensure that their team always has the upper hand. They are the first to step up when a show of strength is needed and the one everyone looks to when negotiations take a hard turn. Their brutal charisma makes them both a social and physical powerhouse, capable of leading through intimidation and crushing opposition through raw strength.

Moves

Terrifying Presence

When you attempt to intimidate or cow someone into submission, roll +CHA.

  • On a 10+: they are completely terrified, doing exactly what you want without hesitation. They may even give up additional information or resources to appease you.
  • 7-9: they’re frightened, but they require something in return or have reservations that could cause complications later.
  • 6 or less: they resist your intimidation, and the situation escalates (e.g., they fight back or call for help).

Brutal Strike

When you use brute force to attack or break something, roll +STR.

  • On a 10+: your strike is devastating, dealing 2d6 damage and stunning the target (or completely destroying an object).
  • 7-9: your strike lands, dealing 1d6 damage, but you leave yourself exposed to a counterattack.
  • 6 or less: you miss or the attack backfires, leaving you off-balance or damaging yourself in the process.

Face-to-Face

When you confront an enemy directly and try to intimidate them in a physical confrontation, roll +CHA.

  • On a 10+: they back down immediately, retreat, or surrender, giving you a decisive advantage.
  • 7-9: they hesitate or comply, but only temporarily. They may regroup or prepare for a counterattack.
  • 6 or less: they fight back fiercely, putting you at risk or escalating the conflict dangerously.

Show of Force

When you demonstrate your physical power to intimidate or control a situation, roll +STR.

  • On a 10+: your show of strength overwhelms those around you. Enemies scatter, or a crowd bends to your will, and your team gains +1 forward.
  • 7-9: your show of force works, but someone steps up to challenge you or takes advantage of your distraction.
  • 6 or less: your display backfires, making you appear reckless or vulnerable, encouraging others to resist or attack.

Crushing Blow

When you land a powerful strike intended to incapacitate, roll +STR

  • On a 10+: your strike deals massive damage (2d6) and leaves the enemy incapacitated for the next round.
  • 7-9: your strike hits hard (1d6), but the enemy can still fight back, albeit weakened.
  • 6 or less: the attack backfires, leaving you open to retaliation or causing unintended consequences.

Fearless Leader

When you use your intimidating presence to rally your team or inspire them to fight harder, roll +CHA

  • On a 10+:your team is emboldened by your strength and gains +1 forward on their next actions.
  • 7-9: they are inspired, but a complication arises (e.g., someone questions your tactics or takes unnecessary risks).
  • 6 or less: your leadership fails, and the team loses confidence, suffering -1 forward on their next actions.

Juice

(Fear Control & Physical Dominance)

The Intimidator builds Juice through fear control & physical dominance. They are known for using their raw strength and intimidating personality to bend others to their will.

  • Fear Control Boost: When you successfully intimidate or cow someone into submission, gain +1 Juice.

  • Physical Dominance: When you overpower an enemy in a physical confrontation, gain +1 Juice.

Weapons & Gear

  • Reinforced Knuckles: Specially reinforced knuckle guards for dealing heavy blows in combat. (Damage: 1d6, Close)

  • Heavy Club: A brutal, simple weapon designed to intimidate and crush. (Damage: 1d8, Close, Intimidating)

  • Threatening Armor: Armor designed not just for protection but for intimidation, with spikes, symbols of power, and a fearsome look. Grants +1 Armor and +1 forward when attempting to intimidate others.

  • Rope or Chains: Useful for restraining enemies or making a point through physical coercion. Grants +1 forward when capturing or restraining someone.

Spectres (Special Moves)

Aura of Fear

Once per session: you can radiate an aura of pure fear. Anyone in your immediate vicinity (within sight) is overwhelmed by your presence and must either flee, freeze, or submit.

  • Enemies affected by this aura gain -1 forward on any actions they attempt while under its influence.

    Iron Flock

    Unlike the Eyes in the Sky, your drones aren’t about precision—they’re about pressure. You have a special set of combat drones designed to overwhelm, crush, and instill fear. Choose two (2) drones to deploy during a single hunt:

    • Execution (Heavy Assault Drone) A walking gun platform with a high-powered shotgun or rotary cannon (depending on the one you purchase). It suppresses enemy positions and makes them think twice before peeking out.
    • The Hound (Tracker Drone) This drone hunts a target relentlessly, emitting a deep, unnerving mechanical growl. Targets pursued by the Hound suffer disadvantage on stealth and escape attempts.
    • The Sweep (Shockwave Drone): Deploys with a loud, concussive blast, throwing enemies off balance and knocking weaker ones off their feet. Breaks defensive lines with ease.
    • Get Over Here (Restraint Drone): a counter-capture drone that launches out mechanical tendrils to bind, choke, or pin a target in place. Can be used to take someone alive or to crush them into unconsciousness.
    • Ruckus (Terror Swarm): Releases a cloud of micro-drones that create intense noise, flashing lights, and eerie distorted voices - disorienting enemies and making them easier to inimidate or control.

    Once per hunt, you can use a JUICE point and unleash both of your drones in a terrifying full-force asssault, overwhelming an area. Targets affected must eithr flee, surrender, or take a brutal beating.

    The Predator’s Gaze

    Your Membrane Map doesn’t just track the Hunt—it projects your presence into the minds of those caught within it. Through a combination of biometric scans, behavioral prediction, and psychological warfare, you can make enemies feel like they’re already cornered.

    When you activate Predator’s Gaze, roll +CHA

    • On a 10+ , every enemy within range immediately senses your presence, feeling hunted, exposed, and vulnerable.

      • You gain three tactical advantages to use however you see fit:

      • forcing an enemy to surrender,
      • revealing hidden foes,
      • making a target freeze up,
      • psychologically pressuring someone into making a mistake.

    • 7-9 you get two advantages, but your intense projection draws attention—an enemy resists harder or focuses on you in panic-fueled desperation.
    • 6 or less the system overloads, glitches, or backfires, making your allies momentarily uneasy or giving false information.

    While the Predator's Gaze is active, the Team gain +1 to intimidation-based actions, as the targets are already feeling the weight of you looming presence.
