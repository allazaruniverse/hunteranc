The Old Head (Comm: CHA/INT)

the wise mentor, the experienced veteran, the one who has seen it all...

"I've seen some shit... Let me tell you how it ends."

Overview

The Old Head is the wise mentor, the experienced veteran, and the one who has seen it all. They’ve been through countless missions, faced untold dangers, and learned valuable lessons along the way. They use their charisma to lead and inspire the team, while their intellect guides them in avoiding costly mistakes.

With age and experience comes the ability to predict outcomes, calm tense situations, and make tough calls when it counts. The Old Head is a character who can look at a situation and know exactly what’s about to go wrong, often preventing disaster or turning the tide through leadership and foresight.

In play, The Old Head is a leader who uses experience and charisma to guide the team through dangerous situations. Whether it’s calming nerves, offering advice, or making contingency plans, they ensure the team doesn’t panic or rush into mistakes. While others may focus on fighting or gathering intel, the Old Head is the one who sees the bigger picture and makes sure the team is prepared for whatever comes their way.

Moves

Voice of Expericence

When you offer guiddance based on past experience, roll +CHA.

  • On a 10+: your advice is invaluable, and the team follows it perfectly, gaining +1 forward on their next action.
  • 7-9: they follow your advice, but there’s a minor complication or misunderstanding (e.g., it takes longer than expected or there’s some pushback).
  • 6 or less: your advice is ignored or misinterpreted, leading to a problematic outcome.

Been There, Done That

When you recall past situations that may be relevant to the issue at hand, roll +INT.

  • On a 10+: you recall exactly how to handle it and gain 3 Juice to spend on any of the following:
    • What are the dangers here?
    • What’s the best way out of this situation?
    • How can I turn this to our advantage?
  • 7-9: you remember enough to avoid disaster, but there’s a complication or unforeseen twist.
  • 6 or less: your memory fails, or the situation is more complex than it seems.

Steady the Nerves

When you calm or inspire others in a tense or dangerous situation roll +CHA.

  • On a 10+: your team is focused and composed, gaining +1 forward for any actions requiring concentration or calm under pressure.
  • 7-9: you keep them calm, but there’s still an underlying tension or fear (e.g., they hesitate or need reassurance).
  • 6 or less: they panic or act out of fear, causing a negative effect (e.g., making a mistake or retreating).

Plan for the Worst

When you make contingency plans for when things inevitably go wrong roll +INT.

  • On a 10+: your plans are rock solid, and you and your team gain an advantage (e.g., knowing an escape route, preparing for a surprise attack).
  • 7-9: your plan works but with an unexpected cost or drawback (e.g., it takes longer, or a resource is expended).
  • 6 or less: your plan backfires or isn’t as foolproof as you thought, putting the team in danger.

Veteran's Call

When you make a strategic decision based on experience, roll +INT

  • On a 10+: your decision proves invaluable, giving your team +1 forward for the remainder of the encounter.
  • 7-9: your decision works but leads to a minor setback or complication.
  • 6 or less: your decision backfires, putting the team in a worse situation.

Last Resort

When you are forced to make a final, desperate call under extreme pressure, roll +CHA

  • On a 10+: your leadership inspires the team, and they execute the plan flawlessly, turning the tide of the situation.
  • 7-9: the plan works, but at a cost (e.g., resources are lost, or a team member is injured).
  • 6 or less: the plan fails, and the situation worsens, but your steady presence still prevents complete chaos.
